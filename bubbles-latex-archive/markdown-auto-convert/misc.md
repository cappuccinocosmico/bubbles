# Exercises

:::note[Exercise]
Given the trigonometric definitions of $\sin$ and $\cos$ derive
$$\begin{aligned}
        \sin(a+b)&=\sin(a)\cos(b) + \cos(a) \sin(b)\\
        \cos(a+b)&=\cos(a)\cos(b) - \sin(a) \sin(b)\\
    
\end{aligned}$$
:::

:::danger[Answer]
$$\begin{aligned}
        \sin(a+b) &= \frac{e^{i(a+b)}-e^{-ix(a+b)}}{2i}\\
        &= \frac{e^{ia}e^{ib}-e^{-ia}e^{-ib}{2i}\\
    
\end{aligned}$$
:::
